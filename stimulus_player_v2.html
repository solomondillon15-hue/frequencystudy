⚠️ Before you begin — Camera & Data Use Notice

This study uses your device's camera and optional gaze tracking to record facial expressions and eye movements during audio stimulus exposure. All processing happens entirely in your browser — no video is recorded, stored, or transmitted externally. Only numerical emotion scores and gaze coordinates are logged alongside your ratings.

📷 Your camera will activate when the study begins. A live feed will be visible to you on-screen during each stimulus. You may decline camera use and complete the study with ratings only.

📋 Study Overview

This tool plays ten audio stimuli — five pure sine tones and five matched consonant triads — across frequency ranges from 79 Hz to 639 Hz. After each stimulus, rate your emotional response.

  1. Wear headphones if possible. Use a quiet environment.
  2. Each stimulus lasts 30 seconds. Listen without doing anything else.
  3. After the tone, rate your valence and arousal (1–5).
  4. Stimuli play in randomised order. You cannot skip once started.
  5. Responses are saved automatically and downloadable as CSV.

ℹ️ About this tool

This player was developed as part of an empirical study investigating emotional responses to isolated frequencies and consonant triads (UWE Bristol, UFCFHQ-45-3, 2025–26). Audio is synthesised via the Web Audio API. Facial analysis via face-api.js (Vincent Mühler, MIT licence). No data is transmitted externally.
